My Font

For this task we had to write our name in a typeface that represented us. I like to work, whether that means on my university assignments or on app development (the field that I work in). If I miss a lecture, I am normally working on catching up on the lecture, assignments or my external ventures. However, although I consider myself to be professional in a working environment, I like to go to parties and have fun in a social capacity. Therefore, I wanted to choose a font that had the following attributes: (clear circle bullet points).

  • Professional
  • Fun
  • Sans-Serif
  • Clean

ref: Cocogoose Pro | Heavitas | Quanitfy | Quartzo

The reason why I wanted a sans-serif font was because most sans-serif fonts convey an “aged” message. As typefaces have evolved over time, the typefaces that we associate with modern and new are the clean sans-serif ones (i.e. Helvetica or Gotham). However, those kinds of typefaces also convey uptight message. Because they are very uniform and use simple geometric shapes (mostly circles and squares). Therefore, they are commonly used in corporate environments. The fonts I chose have the uniformity and cleanliness of Helvetica style typefaces but have rounded corners or small dissimilarities that convey “fun but not chaotic”.

I chose 4 fonts that represented me because it would allow me to pick the one that I liked the best visually, not just because it satisfies the criteria set.

My final choice was Quantify









Brand Font

The Walt Disney logo (left) typeface is inspired from Disney's own signiture (right). The typeface uses smooth curves and a hand-written style to convey a child-like and playful tone. The typeface itself is quite creative with it's unique "t","D" and "y" design. However, I feel that this does not reflect the company as it stands. I find that as Disney the company has increased its reach and power in the creative industry it focuses more on profit and less on the art of what they create. My personal opinion is that the newer disney movies that come out are created using a cookie cutter style that panders to the audience. For example the later Pirates of the Caribean movies and the Cars franchise and the live action adaptations of their previous movies (Beauty and the Beast and Jungle Book).

For this reason, I chose a stencil font (Street Stencil) that reflects their current cookie-cutter attitude.
